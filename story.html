Our Story

In a land called Vvardenfell, there was a boy named Furium who toiled day after day, mud hardening on his boots, the sun relentless on his skin. Dreams of something more, someone more, danced in his heart. One dusk, defiance whispered in his ear, and he slipped past the weathered fence towards the whispering forest.

Deep within, a clearing glowed with the light of a thousand fireflies. And there, amidst wildflowers, sat Sa'avi. Not a princess in finery, but a girl made of starlight and summer rain. Her eyes met his, and the forest held its breath.

Sa'avi knew the night sky like her own hand, the dance of stars her only escape from the troll's ramshackle hut. His crooked smile mocked her from the shadows, always close. Yet, with Furium... it was a moment just for them.

Days turned into stolen meetings under the old oak, Furium risking his chores and Sa'avi, her captor's wrath. He'd bring wildflowers, and she'd read him tales in starlight.

Then, a night when the moon was but a sliver, Sa'avi deciphered a change in the cards – an opening, a path. With a hurried whisper and desperate hope, she slipped past the slumbering troll, towards the forest where Furium waited. No grand battles, just the hushed beat of running hearts, a hand clasped tight, and a dawn that tasted of freedom.

Fear faded with each step further from the troll's domain. Furium, hand in Sa'avi's, found not a grand castle, but a hillside burrow bathed in golden afternoon light. It was small, earthen, perfect.

Sa'avi, who dreamt of starlight and whispered fates, now marveled at the way rain pattered upon budding leaves. Furium, mud-stained boy turned gentle protector, transformed the soil with calloused hands. Seeds sprouted, vines twined, and with every blossom, Sa'avi's smile rivaled the sun.

News of the girl who vanished drifted back to the troll, like the sting of a dying wasp. Yet, forest paths twisted and turned, and his cunning was no match for the sanctuary they'd built.

Years passed in a symphony of birdsong and harvests. Furium's simple farm knowledge bloomed into a haven of fruits and vegetables – Sa'avi's dream, made real by his unwavering love. Their hobbit home was filled with warmth, with the laughter that had long eluded them.

And so, the farm boy who yearned for love, and the girl who read the stars, found their happily ever after. Not in a kingdom, but in a garden of their own making, born from courage and a simple, steadfast heart.
